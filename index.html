the Philippines

"Hidden wonders" or lesser-known tourist destinations in the Philippines include places like El Nido Palawan, Albay in Bicol, Chocolate Hills in Bohol, Enchanted River in Surigao del Sur, and Calaguas Islands in Camarines Norte. These places offer travelers unique experiences and attractions that are off the beaten path, showcasing the natural beauty and rich culture of the Philippines.

HISTORY

Chocolate Hills

The history of the Bohol Chocolate Hills is shrouded in mystery and legend. According to local folklore, the hills were formed by two giants who engaged in a battle that lasted for days, hurling boulders and rocks at each other. When they were exhausted and could no longer fight, they made amends and left the island, leaving behind the hills as a testament to their strength and bravery. However, the scientific explanation for the formation of the Chocolate Hills is that they are made up of limestone that was deposited underwater millions of years ago. Over time, the limestone was uplifted and eroded by rain and other natural forces, resulting in the unique cone-shaped hills that we see today. Regardless of their origin, the Chocolate Hills have become an important part of Bohol's cultural and natural heritage, and are recognized as a National Geological Monument by the Philippine government. They continue to attract tourists from all over the world who are drawn to their striking beauty and mysterious origins.

Mayon Volcano

Mayon Volcano is an active stratovolcano located in the province of Albay in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. The volcano has erupted numerous times since the 17th century, with the most recent major eruption occurring in 2018. Mayon Volcano is named after the legendary princess Daragang Magayon, and has become a popular tourist attraction due to its spectacular beauty. Despite the dangers posed by the volcano, the local government has developed the area around it, building viewing decks and improving access roads. Mayon Volcano was declared a National Park in 1938, and designated as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 2003, in recognition of its cultural and natural significance.

El nido palawan

El Nido is a coastal town located in the northern part of Palawan, Philippines. It is known for its beautiful beaches, towering limestone cliffs, and crystal-clear waters. The history of El Nido dates back to the pre-colonial period, when it was inhabited by the Cuyunon people. During the Spanish colonization, the town became a trading post for goods such as rice, corn, and cotton. In the early 20th century, El Nido became a hub for pearl diving and fishing, attracting migrants from various parts of the Philippines. During World War II, the town was occupied by the Japanese, who used it as a military base. After the war, El Nido remained a small fishing village until the 1970s, when it was discovered by backpackers and became a popular tourist destination. Today, El Nido is a thriving tourist town that attracts visitors from all over the world. Its natural beauty and rich history make it a unique and fascinating destination for travelers.
